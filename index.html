Anwar Gargash reassures UAE’s support to Sudan

February 13, 2020February 13, 2020 Edward Yang Uncategorized

The United Arab Emirates has emphasized its full dedication in helping Sudan’s initiatives. UAE Foreign Minister Anwar Gargash landed in Khartoum y for his two-day visit to the country. He met with Lieutenant General Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo, Deputy Head of the Transitional Military Council (TMC) and a number of ministers in the transitional government.

Following his meetings with Sudanese Finance Minister Ibrahim el-Badawi, Gargash reiterated the UAE government’s interest in maintaining relations with Sudan. In an interview, he said, “The UAE is fully committed to Sudan’s success and to its economic and regional take-off to be able to play its natural role as one of the biggest and most important Arab countries”.

He referred to the company’s historical linkages and shared interests between the UAE and Sudan, especially its geostrategic and economic relations.

The UAE minister asserted that his visit is part of a series of shared visits to address various economic and political issues between officials from both nations. He is set to meet with different officials from Sudan to talk about ways of improving bilateral ties. He also showed pride in the Sudanese population of the UAE, which is about 100,000 people, commending their contributions to his country’s history.

Gargash also arranged a meeting with the Under-Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Ambassador al-Siddiq Abdul-Aziz, to address ways to enhance bilateral relations. Both parties have addressed the problems of common concern and development in the Arab and North African areas, the foreign ministry said.
